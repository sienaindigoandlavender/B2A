## Summary

Search engines directed humans to websites; AI agents complete tasks directly. The implication for businesses is that the distribution channel is shifting from "be findable by humans" to "be callable by agents."

## The evidence

- 94% of B2B buyers already use LLMs during their buying journey (6sense, 2025 Buyer Experience Report)
- IBM iX projects 75% of digital engagements will begin inside agent environments by 2027
- ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude now appear as referrers in website analytics — a new category of traffic source
- Google's Search Generative Experience is increasingly answering queries without a click-through

## What changes

In search-driven discovery, the human clicks to your website and evaluates. In agent-driven discovery, the agent evaluates before (or instead of) the human ever seeing your website. The human gets a recommendation or a completed transaction, not a list of links.

This means: visibility in the agent's consideration set is more valuable than a high search ranking, because it is closer to the point of transaction.

## Implications

- `llms.txt` and structured data become the equivalent of meta tags and title tags — the signals agents use to understand what you offer
- Content quality for machines (factual, structured, unambiguous) matters more than content optimised for human engagement
- The window for establishing agent visibility is open now; by 2027, default agent consideration sets may be well-established

## Open questions

- Is agent discovery winner-take-all (agents always use the same sources) or diverse?
- How do smaller businesses compete for agent visibility against large platforms with better API infrastructure?
- Does agent-driven discovery reduce or increase price competition?
