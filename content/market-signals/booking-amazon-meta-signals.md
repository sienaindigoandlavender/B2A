## Amazon

Amazon is testing Rufus (research agent) and Alexa agents for autonomous purchasing within the Prime ecosystem. The structural advantage: Amazon already has the world's largest structured product catalog, real-time inventory data, and established payment authorisation infrastructure. Amazon becoming agent-ready is largely a matter of exposing existing infrastructure to agent interfaces — which is why analysts project it as one of the clearest early B2A winners.

## Booking.com

Booking.com operates API-first by design — its entire business model is built around programmatic travel inventory access. It is structurally better positioned for B2A than most of its category because its data is already structured for machine consumption. The transition from "travel agencies call our API" to "AI agents call our API" is incremental, not transformational.

## Meta

Meta's AI assistant can place ads and transact across WhatsApp and Instagram Shops. The structural advantage: Meta's data often knows user preferences better than any other platform, giving its agents a personalisation edge in A2C interactions. The commercial model (transaction fees + ad revenue from agent-mediated discovery) maps cleanly onto its existing business.

## What these signal

The platforms building agent-mediated purchasing first are those with: existing structured product data, existing programmatic API infrastructure, and an existing relationship with consumers that creates trust for delegation. The structural advantage accrues to early API-first businesses — which is a strong argument for building agent-accessible infrastructure now, not later.
