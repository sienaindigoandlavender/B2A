## Where the moat is NOT

**The agent itself.** Building a capable AI agent is increasingly commoditised. The underlying models (GPT-5, Claude, Gemini) are accessible to everyone. Agent frameworks (LangChain, LangGraph) are open-source. The agent layer has no structural moat.

**Generic structured data.** Publishing schema.org markup and an OpenAPI spec is table stakes. It creates parity, not advantage.

**Being on a major platform.** Being accessible via Amazon or Google's agent ecosystem gives distribution, but the platform captures most of the margin and all of the relationship.

## Where the moat might be

**Domain-specific structured knowledge.** The agent that knows which riads in Marrakech are actually good (not just reviewed well on platforms) has an information edge that cannot be extracted from training data. This edge only holds if: (a) the knowledge is maintained, (b) it is structured in a way agents can access, and (c) the owner controls distribution.

**Trust infrastructure.** Businesses that establish verifiable, consistent structured data early will develop reputations among agent systems for reliability. This is speculative — it requires agents to actually track and weight the reliability of data sources — but the direction is plausible.

**Policy tooling as a product.** If machine-readable policy becomes a standard, whoever builds the easiest tooling for creating and maintaining policy files gains adoption. Network effects are limited, but switching costs (policies are already structured in your format) create some stickiness.

**Vertical depth.** A platform that is the authoritative, agent-accessible resource for luxury Morocco travel — with structured itineraries, structured pricing, structured policies, verified content — is more defensible than a general platform because the depth is expensive to replicate.

## The honest answer

I don't know where the moat is yet. The agent economy is moving too fast to make high-confidence claims about durable advantage. This is a page I expect to revise significantly over the next 12 months.
