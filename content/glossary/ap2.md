An open protocol for agent-initiated financial transactions, announced by Google in September 2025 in collaboration with Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, Stripe, and others. Built as an extension of both MCP (tool layer) and A2A (coordination layer).

AP2 addresses the core problem with applying legacy payment infrastructure to autonomous agents: traditional systems require a human to be present at the moment of payment. Agents operate asynchronously.

AP2's key mechanisms: **delegated authorisation** (human pre-authorises agent within defined limits), **transaction anchoring** (each transaction cryptographically linked to original consent), and **liability attribution** (records who is responsible at each step). Payment-method-agnostic.
