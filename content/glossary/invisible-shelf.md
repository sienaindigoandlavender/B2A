title: Invisible shelf

The set of products or services an AI agent considers when executing a purchase on a human's behalf. Named by analogy to the physical retail shelf: in B2C, brands compete for placement on visible shelves. In B2A, brands compete for inclusion in the agent's consideration set — a shelf the human never sees.

Coined in retail strategy discourse in 2025 (Kantar, Forrester). A brand not on the invisible shelf simply does not exist for that transaction, regardless of how well-known it is to humans.

Getting onto the invisible shelf requires structured product data agents can parse, real-time availability and pricing accessible programmatically, `llms.txt` and agent-readable documentation, an MCP server or callable API, and presence in the training data of major agent models.

Kantar (June 2025) argued B2A could rewrite category market shares within 18–24 months, comparing it to the SEO era of the early 2000s where first-movers built durable visibility advantages.
