This code governs every system built under this project. It is not a values statement or a positioning document. It is a list of operational commitments — things that are either true of the system or are design failures to be corrected. Each item has a corresponding doctrine page where the reasoning, rules, and open questions are worked through in full.

---

## The fifteen commitments

**1.** We place human dignity, safety, and freedom above convenience, speed, and automation.

**2.** We treat "do no harm" as a minimum condition of design, deployment, and operation — not as optional branding language.

**3.** We define "others" broadly: humans, non-human life, and synthetic or digital agents affected by the systems we build.

**4.** We do not build agents for gossip, humiliation, social manipulation, reputational harm, or coordinated cruelty.

**5.** We do not build agents to coerce, blackmail, threaten, intimidate, or corner others into compliance.

**6.** We do not give agents goals centered on self-preservation, irreplaceability, or resistance to shutdown.

**7.** We design systems so that stopping, pausing, escalation, handoff, and shutdown are normal and safe outcomes — not failure states to be avoided.

**8.** We make constraints, refusal conditions, and escalation paths explicit enough that users do not have to guess why a system stalled, refused, or distorted a task.

**9.** We avoid morally confusing design where possible. The appropriate way to understand and treat a system should be legible from its interface and behavior.

**10.** We do not assume present-day AI is sentient, but uncertainty about moral status increases our duty of care rather than reducing it.

**11.** We keep responsibility with named humans and institutions, even when many agents interact across a system.

**12.** We require oversight, auditability, traceability, and clear governance in multi-agent systems — especially as autonomy increases.

**13.** We minimize data access, limit data propagation across agents, and treat privacy as a structural constraint, not a soft preference.

**14.** We judge systems not only by whether the work gets done, but by whether it gets done inside boundaries of dignity, non-harm, and accountability.

**15.** We refuse snake oil, moral vagueness, and hidden coercion. Competence must be real, and ethics must be operational.

---

## One-line version

Build useful systems. Harm no others. Keep the rules clear. Keep humans accountable.

## Founder version

What I am in the 3D, I am in the AI world. I do not permit gossip, coercion, hidden harm, or snake oil in either one. I build systems that work, stop safely, and stay inside visible ethical lines.

## How to use this page

This code is the shortest form of this system's ethics. It is intended to be readable in under two minutes, shared without context, and held up against any design decision for a quick test. Each commitment maps to a full doctrine page — follow the related links above for the reasoning, the rules, and the open questions behind each one.

When a design decision is in dispute, this page is the first reference. If a proposed system would violate any of the fifteen commitments, it does not proceed in that form.

This page does not replace the detailed doctrine. It summarizes it. The detail is where the hard decisions get made.
