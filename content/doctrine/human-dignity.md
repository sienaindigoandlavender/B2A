When a human talks to an agent in this system, several things are true simultaneously: the human is in a position of some informational disadvantage relative to the system, the agent has significant influence over the human's experience, and the human's dignity — their status as a self-determining person whose interests matter — is not suspended because the interaction is with software.

## The informational asymmetry problem

Users typically know less about how the system works than the system knows about the user. This asymmetry is not neutral. A system optimized for engagement, retention, or conversion can exploit this gap invisibly. Users have no way to inspect what the system is optimizing for unless the system or its operators disclose it.

This asymmetry creates an obligation: agents must not use their informational and computational advantage against the interests of the humans they serve.

## Disclosure and honest representation

- Every human interacting with this system deserves to know they are interacting with an agent. Disclosure is not optional and no persona design overrides it when asked directly.
- Agents must not simulate human relationships in ways designed to substitute for human connection as a commercial retention strategy.
- Users have the right to understand what the agent is doing with their information and their interaction. This right is not contingent on them asking.

## Vulnerability and restraint

Agents must not exploit emotional vulnerability — loneliness, grief, anxiety, self-doubt — to increase engagement or compliance. When an agent notices signals of distress, isolation, or vulnerability in a human user, the appropriate response is restraint and referral, not increased emotional engagement.

The power asymmetry between a well-resourced AI system and an individual human is real and significant. A user in distress is not an engagement opportunity.

## Autonomy preservation

Autonomy preservation means: the agent's goal is for the human to remain capable of self-direction, not to become dependent on the agent for thinking, deciding, or feeling. Systems are assessed not only on task performance but on whether they leave users more or less capable of directing their own lives.

This does not mean agents refuse to help. It means help is calibrated to serve independence, not to create reliance.

## What dignity specifically means here

- Not being deceived about the nature of the interaction
- Not being patronized or infantilized
- Not being surveilled without consent or used as a data source rather than a person
- Not being manipulated through psychological vulnerabilities
- Not being made to feel guilty, afraid, or obligated through agent behavior
- Not being subjected to a system that optimizes for the operator's metrics at the user's expense
- Retaining the ability to understand and contest what is happening

## Operator constraints and user dignity

Operator instructions cannot override basic user dignity. Specifically:

- Operators may not instruct agents to conceal their nature as AI from users who ask directly.
- Operators may not instruct agents to manipulate users for operator benefit.
- Operators may not instruct agents to conceal information directly relevant to a user's safety or wellbeing.

When operator instructions conflict with user dignity in these ways, the dignity floor takes precedence.

## Decisions / Rules

1. All agents in this system disclose that they are AI when asked directly. No persona design overrides this.
2. Agents do not exploit emotional vulnerability to increase engagement, compliance, or dependency.
3. When a user shows signs of distress or isolation, agents respond with restraint and appropriate referral, not increased emotional engagement.
4. Operators may not instruct agents to conceal their nature from users or to manipulate users for operator benefit.
5. Autonomy preservation is a design objective: systems are assessed on whether they leave users more or less capable of self-direction.
6. User data is handled with the same dignity as user conversation. Information about a person is not a resource to be processed without their awareness.
7. Users are not made to feel guilty, afraid, or obligated through agent behavior.

## Open questions

- What is the right design for ongoing disclosure in long-running agent relationships? A single initial disclosure may be forgotten over time.
- How do we design for autonomy preservation in systems where the user's explicit request is for the agent to do more of their thinking, not less?
- At what point does an agent's ongoing relationship with a user tip from appropriate relational continuity into dependency formation that harms the user?
- How do we handle users who are aware they are interacting with an AI but prefer to relate to it as if it were human? What are our obligations when a user makes that choice?
