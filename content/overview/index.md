This wiki is a personal knowledge base on Business-to-Agent (B2A) commerce and the emerging agent economy. It is built for thinking, not for publishing. The goal is to structure what I know, surface what I don't, and develop original theses about where this is going.

## What this wiki covers

The agent economy is the shift from human-initiated digital activity to agent-initiated activity — where AI systems act autonomously on behalf of humans to discover, evaluate, and transact. B2A is the commercial model that sits at the centre of this shift: businesses building products and infrastructure for AI agents as customers.

This wiki covers the concept (what it is), the evidence (market signals), the opportunity (business models), the mechanics (protocols and implementation), the strategic edge (policy layer), and my own thinking (theses and experiments).

## How to navigate

Start with **why-b2a** if you are new to the concept. Read **working-thesis** to understand what this project is actually trying to figure out. Use the section index at the top to move between topics.

Pages have status tags: `seed` means early notes; `active` means being developed; `strong-signal` means well-evidenced; `speculative` means interesting but unproven; `dead-end` means explored and set aside.
