## The one-sentence version

Every B2C and B2B business model was built on the assumption that a human makes the purchasing decision. That assumption is breaking.

## The structural argument

B2C optimises for human attention: visual design, emotional resonance, SEO, social proof. B2B optimises for human relationships: sales teams, RFPs, demos, procurement committees. Both are built around the cognitive and social patterns of human buyers.

AI agents do not have attention. They do not feel brand affinity. They do not respond to visual hierarchy. They evaluate criteria, call APIs, and execute. A business that has spent a decade optimising for human buyers has built infrastructure that is largely irrelevant to an agent.

This is not a marginal shift. It is a structural one — comparable to the shift from physical retail to e-commerce, or from print advertising to search. In each case, the underlying logic of commerce changed, and businesses that understood the new logic early captured durable advantages.

## Why now specifically

Three things converged in 2024–2025 that moved B2A from theory to deployment:

**Capable agents.** LLMs crossed a reliability threshold. OpenAI's Operator, Google's Gemini agents, and Claude can now execute multi-step commercial tasks without constant human correction.

**Payment rails.** OpenAI and Stripe launched in-conversation checkout in September 2025. Google announced the Agent Payments Protocol (AP2). Visa, Mastercard, and PayPal all demoed agent-ready APIs. The financial plumbing for autonomous transactions now exists.

**Protocol standards.** MCP (Model Context Protocol) gave agents a universal interface to external services. With 97 million monthly SDK downloads by late 2025, it became the de facto standard faster than almost any prior developer protocol.

## Why most businesses are not ready

The default digital business is built for human interaction: a website that humans browse, a checkout that humans complete, a support system that humans navigate. None of this is agent-accessible by default.

Being agent-accessible requires: a structured product catalog with machine-readable attributes, a real-time pricing and availability API, an MCP server or equivalent tool interface, an `llms.txt` file that guides agents to the right content, and an authorisation framework for delegated purchasing.

Most businesses have none of these. The window to build first-mover infrastructure is open now.

## Open questions

- At what autonomy level do consumers become comfortable delegating purchasing? The answer differs by category and by generation.
- How does brand value transfer to an agent world? If an agent is choosing between options, does prior brand equity affect the decision at all?
- Does B2A advantage compound (like SEO) or commoditise quickly (like price comparison sites)?
