## What this is not

**Not a comprehensive survey of AI.** This wiki is not about LLMs, model architecture, training methods, or AI capabilities in general. It is specifically about the commercial and structural implications of AI agents acting as economic actors.

**Not a news aggregator.** The market signals section documents evidence, but the goal is analysis and synthesis, not comprehensiveness. Important developments that do not change the underlying thesis do not need to be tracked here.

**Not a product spec.** The implementation and experiments sections document thinking and prototypes, but this is not an engineering specification or a product roadmap in a formal sense.

**Not a public document.** This is written for personal thinking. It is allowed to be uncertain, wrong, and contradictory. Pages marked `speculative` or `dead-end` are not embarrassments — they are part of the process.

**Not about building an AI agent.** The thesis here is about structuring businesses for agent-mediated commerce, not about building the agents themselves. The agent layer is a commodity. The policy and structure layer is the opportunity.

## Scope boundaries

| In scope | Out of scope |
|---|---|
| How businesses become agent-accessible | How to train or fine-tune LLMs |
| Policy and decision layer design | Agent reasoning and planning internals |
| Commercial models for the B2A era | Consumer AI assistant design |
| Specific vertical opportunities (travel, hospitality) | General-purpose AI platforms |
| Small business implementation | Enterprise-scale deployments |
| Protocol standards (MCP, A2A, AP2) | Broader internet infrastructure |
