## Structural questions

- Does the agent economy concentrate power (platforms capture value) or distribute it (small businesses reach agent buyers directly)?
- How does brand equity transfer — or not — into an agent-mediated world?
- Is agent visibility winner-take-all per category, or does it remain fragmented?

## Technical questions

- What is the minimum viable policy format that an agent can reliably reason about?
- How do you handle combinatorial complexity in policy files (many interacting rules)?
- How does an agent verify that a published policy file is accurate and not manipulated?
- What is the right trust and authentication model for agent-to-business transactions?

## Business questions

- Is policy tooling a B2B product, an open standard, or both?
- Which verticals are most ready for agent-mediated purchasing in the next 12 months?
- What is the go-to-market for a policy-layer product?
- Does regional intelligence (e.g., deep Morocco travel knowledge) constitute a durable moat, or does it get absorbed into general agent training?

## Consumer behaviour questions

- At what transaction value do consumers become unwilling to delegate to an agent?
- How does trust in agent-mediated purchases develop over time?
- Does the comfort with delegation differ significantly by generation or by category?
- What happens to aspirational and luxury categories — do consumers still want to be involved, even when delegation is possible?

## Timing questions

- How long is the first-mover window before agent consideration sets calcify?
- When does fully autonomous purchasing (no human confirmation) become normal for complex purchases like travel?
- What is the regulatory response, and on what timeline?
